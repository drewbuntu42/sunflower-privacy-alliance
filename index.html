Our Mission

We investigate surveillance. We publish findings. We demand accountability.

Government surveillance infrastructure is expanding across Kansas with minimal public debate, opaque contracts, and data retention policies that citizens have never been allowed to scrutinize. Automatic license plate readers log your commute. Data brokers sell your movements to agencies that couldn't obtain them through a warrant.

The Sunflower Privacy Alliance is a Wichita-based coalition of researchers, advocates, and concerned Kansans who believe the Fourth Amendment isn't optional. We file public records requests. We show up to city council. We publish everything we find — with the source documents attached.

We are nonpartisan. We are methodical. We are not going away.

01

Investigate

FOIA and KORA requests, city council hearings, and documentation of what agencies prefer to keep opaque.

02

Publish

Every finding published with source documents. No paywalls, no embargoes. The public paid for these records.

03

Advocate

Model ordinances, coordinated testimony, and sustained pressure until accountability exists in law and in practice.

Active Campaigns

Active Investigation

Demand Accountability for Wichita's ALPR Network

WPD operates 191 Flock Safety license plate readers at ~$2,500 per camera per year, funded through its own software budget after the original DOJ grant expired. We filed a Kansas Open Records Act request for audit logs showing who accessed the data and when. The request was denied. WPD has since acknowledged publicly that they lack the internal capacity to audit the system themselves. A $478,000 surveillance network with no independent oversight and no public access to records is a system operating on trust alone — and trust must be earned.

In Development

Model Surveillance Oversight Ordinance

Data Broker Procurement Audit

Kansas state and local agencies are purchasing commercial data — location history, behavioral profiles, purchase records — from data brokers, effectively bypassing the Fourth Amendment warrant requirements that would apply to direct collection. We are mapping these contracts.
